Me

After 8 years in the management consultancy and project management sectors, I needed a new career challenge. I looked back over my career to work out what I truly enjoyed. Speaking to people that had worked with me I soon discovered what I really loved; problem solving, logic, being creative, having structure to my work and something to show for my work. This is where my coding journey began and after completing various online coding courses I signed up to do the Web Development Immersive at General Assembly (GA).

I have always loved learning and developing new ideas, seeing them come to fruition. Whether that be delivering complex infrastructure projects during my time working in the Engineering & Construction sector, or building a well-designed customer focused application as part of my course at GA. These experiences have developed my skills in effective team working, clear communication and project management. My desire to learn continues as I seek to work for an organisation that shares my values and allows me to develop in a fast-paced and exciting environment.

Hidden Gems - MERN full stack application

A seven day group project to build a full stack MERN application as part of the General Assembly Web Development Immersive course. The app was based on hidden gems, undiscovered cultural and creative venues and attractions, where users can find and add new 'Gems'. The application has full user authentication to create a profile for their 'Gems' and 'Trips'.I took ownership of the user profile page where users could follow other users and create new 'Trips' and 'Gems'. To effectively manage workload and resource we used a Kanban in the form of Trello to plan our project. We ran daily team stand ups to understand project progress and blockers.

Bee Social - Full stack application

A seven day paired project to build a full stack web application for my final project as part of the General Assembly Web Development Immersive course. The app is a platform where users can create and discover social events in their local area. For example organise a board game club or arrange a five-a-side football game. From a user’s profile they can find and attend events or follow clubs of interest. I took ownership of the club pages where users could follow a club, discover events the club was hosting and chat with other members of the club. Collaborating with another student allowed me to further develop my agile project management and team working skills.
